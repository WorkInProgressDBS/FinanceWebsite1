Feature 3: Financial News Relevant to You

Stay informed with the latest headlines on interest rates, housing prices, wages, and market trends. These stories are selected to help you make better financial decisions.

📉 Interest Rates
  • Fed Holds Rates Steady for Third Straight Meeting
    The Federal Reserve voted to keep benchmark interest rates unchanged, citing mixed signals on inflation and employment.
    Source: Reuters  |  March 2026
  • Mortgage Rates Dip Below 6.5% for First Time in a Year
    Home loan rates have eased slightly, offering some relief to buyers who have been priced out of the market.
    Source: CNBC  |  February 2026
  • High-Yield Savings Accounts Still Paying Over 4%
    Online banks continue to offer competitive savings rates, making it a great time to grow your emergency fund.
    Source: Bankrate  |  March 2026
💼 Wages & Employment
  • Minimum Wage Increases Take Effect in 12 States
    Several states raised their minimum wages at the start of the year, with some now exceeding $16 per hour.
    Source: AP News  |  January 2026
  • Tech Sector Sees Wage Growth Slow After Years of Gains
    Technology workers are seeing smaller salary increases compared to the boom years of 2021–2023.
    Source: Bloomberg  |  February 2026
  • Job Market Remains Strong — Unemployment Stays at 3.9%
    The labor market continues to show resilience, with steady hiring in healthcare, construction, and retail.
    Source: Bureau of Labor Statistics  |  March 2026
🏠 Housing Prices
  • Home Prices Rose 4.2% Year-Over-Year in Most Major Cities
    Despite cooling demand, limited housing inventory is keeping prices elevated in most metro areas across the country.
    Source: Zillow  |  March 2026
  • Rental Prices Begin to Level Off in Southern Cities
    Cities like Austin and Nashville are seeing rent stabilization after several years of rapid increases.
    Source: Wall Street Journal  |  February 2026
  • First-Time Homebuyer Programs Expand in 8 New States
    Down payment assistance programs and tax credits are now available to more buyers than ever before.
    Source: HUD  |  January 2026
📈 Stock Market
  • S&P 500 Closes at Record High on Strong Earnings Reports
    Major index hits a new all-time high as retail and energy companies report better-than-expected quarterly profits.
    Source: MarketWatch  |  March 2026
  • 401(k) Contribution Limits Raised for 2026
    The IRS increased the annual 401(k) contribution limit to $24,500, giving workers more room to save for retirement.
    Source: IRS.gov  |  January 2026
  • Index Funds Continue to Outperform Actively Managed Funds
    Data shows low-cost index funds remain the top performer for long-term retail investors over any 10-year period.
    Source: Morningstar  |  February 2026
